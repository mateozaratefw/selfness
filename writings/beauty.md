# What beauty is

I came across the ![Buenos Aires IBM building](/images/ibm-building-buenos-aires.webp) and couldn't understand why I've found it so beautiful. Its a boring, grey building but I couldn't stop looking at it. I've started asking myself which things I find beautiful and I arrived some conclusions. I'll ignore traditional beauty as the biggest expression of it is found in **non obvious** items.

To explain it, I discard the subjetiveness as it makes it impossible to study beauty; if you don't believe that beauty is objective, you can't seem to develop a taste. 

**Beauty is something that's being true to itself. Something that excellently serves the purpose it was intended for**. Things that transmit what they were intended to in the cleanest of channels. Please, don't confuse this with minimalism; this ignores complex systems that are one of the most beautiful things humanity has produced. 

Let me give you some examples. 

What's great about the ![IBM building](/images/ibm-building-buenos-aires.webp)? It represents IBM at its fullest; it's a tall, grey, boring, office building. What's more IBM than that? It wouldn't be beautiful if it say "Apple" at the top.

More architecture; brutalism. The best brutalism makes you feel horror and desolation. ![Berlin's brutalist mouse bunker](/images/brutalism) it's an architectural masterpiece because how would you expect an animal testing facility to look like? It would be a horrible building if it were minimalist. I don't think the ![trendy eco-brutalism](/images/concrete-jungle.jpg) it's beautiful. When architecture tries to blend with nature it must do so following the nature lines (check ![organic architecture](/images/casa-organica.webp)). If you want to disrupt nature, build a monolith. Eco-brutalism stays at a bland medium.

Obviously we can also find beauty in playful, joyful things. I believe nothing comes closer to representing that in the tech realm than the ![Apple iMac G3](/images/imac-g3). It's see-trough -- it has nothing to hide, it has a handle so you can move it around as you want (full power, it invites to play) and its colorful! It charms you just to see it, and anyone from a child to an old designer would be happy to have one on their desk. 

On art; Pablo Picasso's ![Le Moulin de la Galette](/images/le-moulin-de-la-galette.jpg). Makes me feel seen. **Makes me feel an outsider**. Not casually, It was painted in his first years in Paris and it represents one of his **first encounters** with high society, when he was not yet Pablo Picasso. When Picasso was an outsider.

A systems example would be the now classic ![SpaceX Raptor evolution](/images/raptor-3-evolution.jpg). The iteration cleaned up the engine, but it's still a crazy complex system; just cleaner (and thus, prettier).

But beauty does not ends in things; if not, how could we explain [SpaceX's fourth Falcon 1 launch](/falcon-1)? The human doing what's here for; **to mold nature as we want**. That's what the 4th launch (epically) represents.

-- 

Why does it seems to get amplified when found in a non-obvious place? 

When people try hard to make something beautiful, they sometimes found themselves following *beauty guidelines* instead of concentrating in answering on the cleanest way possible the question of nature; **what's this for?**
