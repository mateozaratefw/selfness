# What's beauty

Beauty is something that's being true to itself. Something that excellently serves the purpose it was intended for. It's not subjective, one chooses what's it's own **code** is and judges by that. This is mine.

One heuristic to understand my code it's that it finds allure in things that **transmit what they were intended to in the cleanest of channels**. 

Personally, I've found beauty in disturbing, off-putting pieces. Pablo Picasso's ![Le Moulin de la Galette](/images/le-moulin-de-la-galette.jpg) makes me feel seen. **Makes me feel an outsider**. And that feeling is what it was intended to communicate. It was painted on it's first years in Paris and it represents one of his **first encounters** with high society, when he was not yet Pablo Picasso. 

Another example is brutalism. The best brutalism makes you feel horror and desolation. ![Berlin's brutalist mouse bunker](/images/Zentrale-Tierlaboratorien-Maeusebunker-Gerd-Haenska.jpg) it's an architectural masterpiece because how would you expect an animal testing facility to look like? It would be a horrible building if it were rennaicense.

Obviously we can also find beauty in playful, joyful things. I believe nothing comes closer to representing that in the tech realm than the ![Apple iMac G3](/images/iMac-G3_blue.jpeg). It's see-trough -- it has nothing to hide, it has a handle so you can move it around as you want and it's colorful! it charms you just to see it, and anyone from a child to an old IBM manager would be happy to have one on it's desk. 

I've arrived to this definition of beauty after asking myself why I liked the ![Buenos Aires IBM building](/images/ibm-building-buenos-aires.webp) so much. It's just a boring, office building and there is nothing more IBM than that. It even looks like a boring computer. 

I grew up thinking I should love the rennaissence columns and ornaments in a 2010-built home in my cheap neighborhood and I just couldn't find emotion in myself when looking at it. Now I realize that's because **it's fake**.
